Heart Diseases

Summary of Heart Disease Causes

  • Increased levels of cholesterol and triglycerides are a common cause of heart disease.
  • Consuming processed and junk foods contributes to heart disease.
  • Smoking and drinking alcohol increase the risk of heart disease.
  • Lack of exercise is a significant factor in developing heart disease.
  • Atherosclerosis occurs when plaque builds up in the artery walls.
  • Plaque buildup narrows the arteries, restricting blood flow.
  • Blood clots can form, further obstructing blood flow.
  • A blockage of blood flow can result in a heart attack or stroke.

Blood Pressure

Summary of Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

  • Over 100 million individuals in India suffer from hypertension.
  • Blood pressure is determined by the amount of blood the heart pumps and the resistance in the arteries.
  • Higher blood pressure results from increased blood volume and narrower arteries.
  • Increased BP levels above 120/80 mmHg are caused by high salt intake, stress, and obesity.
  • Individuals with hypertension are at a higher risk for other complications like diabetes, heart disease, and stroke.
  • Symptoms such as headaches, shortness of breath, or nosebleeds may occur but are not specific to high blood pressure.
  • These symptoms usually appear only when hypertension reaches a severe or life-threatening stage.

Diabetes

Summary of Type 2 Diabetes

  • India has 61.3 million people suffering from Type 2 diabetes, making it the diabetes capital of the world.
  • Type 2 diabetes is caused by high blood sugar levels due to insulin resistance.
  • The pancreas initially produces extra insulin to compensate, but eventually fails to keep up.
  • Symptoms can be mild and often go unnoticed; approximately 8 million people with diabetes are unaware they have it.
  • Common symptoms include excessive thirst, frequent urination, blurry vision, irritability, tingling or numbness in hands or feet, fatigue, and slow-healing wounds.

Obesity

Summary of Obesity

  • Obesity increases susceptibility to lifestyle diseases like diabetes and heart disease.
  • Obesity is defined as having excess body fat, distinct from being overweight.
  • Obesity develops over time when calorie intake exceeds calorie expenditure.
  • The balance between calories consumed and burned varies for each individual.
  • Young Indians are at a higher risk of obesity due to unhealthy habits such as poor diet, smoking, alcohol use, inactivity, stress, and lack of sleep.
  • Losing even 5 to 10 percent of body weight can help delay or prevent related diseases.
